Questions

No fluff.
Straight answers.

Prices are driven by an on-chain oracle that aggregates real-world football data. Match results, form, injury news, transfer activity, and tournament progression all feed directly into valuations. When Arsenal draw at home and lose their top scorer, ARS-PERP moves. That's the whole point.
Yes. Perpetual markets never close. A manager sacking breaks on a Monday morning? You're trading it. Rumours of a January transfer at midnight? The order book is open. Football doesn't run on business hours and neither do we.
Perpetual markets. Unlike prediction markets where you wait for a binary outcome, perpetuals let you enter and exit positions at any time. You're not waiting for the season to end to realise your P&L. Think dYdX meets football — continuous pricing, real leverage, your call on when to close.
All settlements are on-chain and transparent. Mark price is derived from the oracle feed, not from our internal team. You can verify every settlement independently. We don't have access to your positions after they're opened and we can't change settlement prices. That's the architecture we chose.
Notch is a decentralised protocol. You connect with a wallet. We don't process payments, collect card details, or act as a custodian. Regulatory eligibility varies by jurisdiction and users are responsible for compliance with local laws. Check your region's guidelines around decentralised derivatives.
